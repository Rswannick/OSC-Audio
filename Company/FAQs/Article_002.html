How to allow non Apple App Store apps to install on macOS.

We understand that macOS users may encounter a situation where the app is flagged as potentially unsafe during installation, accompanied by a prompt from the operating system asking for permission to continue. We want to clarify that this is not an indication of any actual safety issue with our app.

The reason behind this flagging behavior is that our app is not yet available on the Apple App Store. As a result, macOS recognizes the app as being from an unidentified developer and presents this warning as a precautionary measure.

We want to assure you that our official Gumroad download page. is safe to use and free from any harmful elements. The flagging and permission prompt are merely due to our app not being distributed through the Apple App Store at this time.

We are actively working towards making our app available on the Apple App Store, which will eliminate this warning and provide a smoother installation experience for macOS users.

In the meantime, if you encounter this flag and permission prompt, you can proceed with the installation by granting the necessary permissions. This will allow you to enjoy all the features and benefits of our app without any concerns. Here is a full guide from Apple Support.

We apologize for any inconvenience caused and appreciate your understanding. If you have any further questions or need assistance, please don't hesitate to contact our support team. We are here to help ensure a seamless experience with our app on macOS.
